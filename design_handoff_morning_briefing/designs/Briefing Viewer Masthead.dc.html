05 — Deep Dive ~6 min

The quiet collapse of inference cost, and who it rewards

Synthesised from 6 sources

For two years the story of frontier models was scale: more parameters, more data, more dollars. This week three independent threads suggest the more consequential story is the opposite — how cheap it is becoming to run a capable model once you have one.

Distillation, speculative decoding and aggressive quantization are compounding. Each alone shaves a fraction; together they bend the serving-cost curve faster than last year’s roadmaps assumed.

“When inference is nearly free, the scarce resource stops being the model and becomes the distribution around it.”

If capability per dollar keeps improving, differentiation migrates toward proprietary data, workflow lock-in and trust — which is why the most interesting moves this quarter were about products, not parameters.

Worth watching: whether incumbents with distribution move faster than challengers with models. History rarely rewards the cleverest architecture over the widest reach.
